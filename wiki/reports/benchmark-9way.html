The dev box is shared (20 vCPU / 31.9 GiB) and the load generator (k6/Chrome) runs ON the same box. High-rps and at-density tail latencies (p95/p99) are load-generator-CPU-bound, NOT stack latency — see scale-slo.md §2 and §5. The 10K zipf run's second-scale p50/p95/p99 are k6/Chrome timeouts, not server latency; the run's value is the SERVER-side facts it nails (1 pool, 30 MiB RSS, 0 evicted, 0 5xx). · A clean 100K LOAD-latency SLO is NOT credibly measurable on this box (scale-slo.md §5). The 100000 tenant-count points are MODELED extrapolations of the serve path (RSS/pools/5xx are functions of concurrent working set, not tenant count, so none grows with N); the 100K load p99 is deliberately omitted, not invented. · Competitor cloud (supabase-cloud, firebase) latency/footprint/cost-per-tenant are mostly 'na': we did not measure their managed cloud and their pricing is per-project/per-usage/per-MAU, not per-tenant. Supabase SELF-HOST is a separate contender we DID measure. · Tier idle footprints (basic/essential/pro/max) here are the MEASURED ram_mib_total from artifacts/footprint-<tier>.json (2026-06-12). They differ slightly from the earlier figures in wiki/cost-analysis.md (a 2026-06-11 run); the artifacts cited here are the authoritative measurement. · 'nanobase' in the user's request is interpreted as our binocle-nano edition (the 5 MB single binary).
